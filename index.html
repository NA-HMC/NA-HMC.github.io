1) Motivation and Method (Figure 1)

Figure 1 overview. Existing iterative-guidance methods can violate manifold feasibility, stochastic MAP methods may overfit measurement noise, and deterministic MAP methods can be trapped in local modes. NA-NHMC performs posterior sampling in the initial noise space and maps samples to images through deterministic DDIM, improving robustness while preserving data-manifold consistency.

2) Abstract

Diffusion models have shown strong performance in inverse problems, but existing approaches often suffer from manifold infeasibility, noise overfitting, or local-mode collapse. We propose Noise-space Hamiltonian Monte Carlo (N-HMC), which treats reverse diffusion as a deterministic mapping from initial Gaussian noise to clean images and performs posterior sampling entirely in the noise space. This formulation keeps proposals on the learned manifold and enables robust posterior exploration. We further introduce Noise-Adaptive N-HMC (NA-NHMC), which marginalizes unknown measurement noise variance and removes task-specific tuning of noise-level hyperparameters. Across linear and nonlinear inverse problems on FFHQ and ImageNet, NA-NHMC delivers strong reconstruction quality and robust behavior under unknown noise.

4) Experimental Results (Key Highlights)

  • Nonlinear inverse problems: On FFHQ (σy=0.05), NA-NHMC achieves the best nonlinear deblurring score (PSNR 27.66, SSIM 0.792, LPIPS 0.249) and strongest phase-retrieval performance (PSNR 19.30, SSIM 0.554, LPIPS 0.482).
  • Unknown noise robustness: With the same hyperparameters used in Gaussian settings, NA-NHMC remains robust under impulse and speckle noise and outperforms prior methods on most metrics.
  • Stability: Compared with guidance-based baselines, NA-NHMC shows lower sensitivity to initialization and better convergence behavior on highly ill-posed tasks.
