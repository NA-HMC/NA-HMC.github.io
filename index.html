Abstract

We propose Noise-space Hamiltonian Monte Carlo (N-HMC), a posterior sampler for inverse problems with pretrained diffusion models. The key idea is to treat reverse diffusion as a deterministic map from initial noise to clean image and perform inference entirely in the initial noise space. This keeps proposals on the learned data manifold, mitigates artifact accumulation, and improves robustness to measurement noise. We further introduce Noise-Adaptive N-HMC (NA-NHMC), which marginalizes unknown measurement noise variance and avoids task-specific hyperparameter tuning. Across linear and nonlinear inverse problems on FFHQ and ImageNet, NA-NHMC achieves strong reconstruction quality and robust behavior under unknown noise.

Motivation and Method

Issue 1: Manifold Infeasibility

Likelihood guidance on intermediate states can move samples off the diffusion manifold.

Issue 2: Noise Overfitting

Image-space MAP optimization may overfit measurements in high or unknown noise regimes.

Issue 3: Local Mode Traps

Deterministic optimization can collapse into one mode on highly ill-posed tasks.

Noise-Space Posterior

p(x_T | y) ∝ p(y | D(x_T)) · p(x_T)
∇ log p(x_T | y) = -x_T + ∇ log p(y | x_T)

NA-NHMC marginalizes unknown Gaussian variance with a Jeffreys prior, yielding a parameter-free likelihood term in noise space.
